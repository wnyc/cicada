Building the DIY Kit


  1. The Brood II bot kit listens for cicada sounds, tests the soil temperature, then sends the data to WNYC.

  2. Parts
    Here are the parts you need to start building your kit:
    • Arduino Uno
    • Thermistor
    • Microphone
    • Wire wrapping tool
    • Electrical tape
    • Pole/stick/stake

  3. First, go shop for materials at a store like the venerable Radioshack.

  4. Most of them have geek sections that carry Arduino kits and support kits.

  5. Put together your kit with care...

  6. ... especially when wrapping wires.

  7. The thermistor is the part that tells the temperature of the soil.

  8. You can test it by putting it on some ice.

  9. Then, do lots of programming of the Arduino. Save your work!

  10. Test the mic after you program the Arduino to tell a cicada from Lady Gaga.

  11. Be sure to hook it up correctly.

  12. Use a visible marker to mark the spot of your Brood II bot.

  13. To plant your bot, first attach the thermistor to the end of the stake.

  14. After it's secured with electrical tape, cover it with a latex glove.

  15. Tape it up nice. Use a piece of colored tape to indicate the 8 inches you'll plant the stake.
